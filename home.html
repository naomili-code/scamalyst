Identifying Non-Legitimate Websites

Beyond scam messages, scammers create fake websites to steal your information. Here's how to spot them:

ğŸ”— 1. Scrutinize the URL (Web Address)

  • Misspelled Domains: Watch for slightly altered names like "micros0ft.com" instead of "microsoft.com" or "gooogle.com"
  • Suspicious Top-Level Domains: Be wary of unusual extensions like .store, .shop, or .top
  • Subdomain Tricks: In a URL like "google.com.cust-login.ie", the actual domain is "cust-login.ie", not google.com
  • Check for HTTPS: Ensure the address bar starts with "https://" (the 's' stands for secure), but remember scammers can sometimes obtain certificates too

âœï¸ 2. Evaluate Site Content and Design

  • Poor Grammar and Spelling: Legitimate companies have professional content. Frequent typos and broken English are red flags
  • Low-Quality Images: Blurry, pixelated logos or product images often indicate a fraudulent site
  • Missing or Generic "About Us": Vague, empty, or formulaic "About Us" pages are signs of a scam
  • No Real Contact Information: Legitimate businesses provide a physical address, phone number, and professional emailâ€”not just generic forms or @gmail.com addresses

ğŸ’° 3. Check for "Too Good to be True" Deals

  • Suspiciously Cheap Products: If a brand-new, popular item is listed at a massive discount (90% off), it's almost certainly a scam to steal credit card information

ğŸ›¡ï¸ 4. Use Security and Verification Tools

  • Check Domain Age: Use WHOIS lookup tools to see when the domain was created. A newly registered domain (weeks old) claiming to be well-established is suspicious
  • Google Safe Browsing: Enter the URL into Google Transparency Report to check its reputation
  • Check for Reviews: Search the website name along with "scam," "reviews," or "complaints" on sites like Trustpilot or BBB

ğŸ“‹ 5. Review Policies

  • No Return Policy: Legitimate e-commerce sites have clear shipping, return, and privacy policies. If these are missing or overly complicated, avoid the site

âš ï¸ Summary of Major Red Flags

  • Payment Methods: Only accepting non-reversible, untraceable payments like cryptocurrencies, wire transfers, or gift cards
  • Urgent Demands: Sites pressuring you to act immediately, claiming your account is locked or a deal is expiring soon
  • Unexpected Pop-ups: Frequent, suspicious pop-ups asking for personal information
