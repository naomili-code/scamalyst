About ScamSensei

Why This Project Exists

AI has made scams easier and more convincing. In 2025‚Äì2026, millions of people fell victim to AI-generated phishing, deepfakes, and social engineering. Young people, immigrants, and non-native English speakers are hit hardest because traditional security training doesn't address AI-specific threats.

ScamSensei asks: What if AI itself could help people recognize when they're being manipulated?

How AI Helps (and Its Limits)

What we use AI for:

  • Pattern recognition (detecting urgency, threats, requests for sensitive info)
  • Linguistic analysis (spotting AI-generated text through word choice and structure)
  • Educational explanations (breaking down why scams work)

What we don't claim:

  • 100% accuracy (scammers evolve; so must we)
  • Replacement for human judgment (context matters)
  • Legal advice or guarantees

Designed for Everyone

Cybersecurity tools are often built for tech experts. ScamSensei is built for everyone:

  • Non-native English speakers: We explain idioms and cultural cues scammers exploit.
  • Older adults: Simple, jargon-free explanations.
  • Students: Learn through practice and real examples.
  • Low-tech users: No sign-ups, no downloads, no complicated settings.

The Ethics of AI Detection

Question: Is it ethical to detect AI-generated text?

Our answer: Yes, when the goal is protection, not surveillance. We use AI detection to help people recognize when they're being manipulated‚Äînot to restrict AI use or spy on people's writing.

We believe:

  • Transparency matters more than secrecy
  • People deserve to know what's real
  • Education beats blame
  • Tools should serve users, not corporations

Limitations

  • Works best on English text (other languages coming)
  • ~70-80% accuracy on typical phishing; lower on novel attacks
  • Doesn't check if links are actually malicious‚Äîjust if they look suspicious
  • Doesn't verify sender identity (that requires DMARC/SPF technical checks)
  • Constantly evolving as scammers adapt
